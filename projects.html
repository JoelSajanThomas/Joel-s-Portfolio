Purchasing Behaviour of Consumable Products by Students

This study examines the purchasing behaviour of college students toward consumable products, with a specific focus on the Pathanamthitta district. The research explores key factors influencing studentsâ€™ buying decisions, including price sensitivity, brand preference, product availability, peer influence, and promotional strategies. Data was collected through structured questionnaires administered to students from various colleges in the district. The findings provide insights into consumption patterns, decision-making factors, and the impact of socio-economic background on purchasing behaviour. The study ultimately aims to help retailers and marketers better understand student consumers and develop strategies that effectively meet their needs.
